WebQuest Two

Searching the World Wide Web

Introduction

In this WebQuest you will review some of the latest information relating to the tools and techniques for finding information on the Internet. Many people use the Internet and they find information by using different tools and strategies. However, the search tools and strategies, like the Internet itself, are changing. Some understanding and knowledge of the best ways and means for searching the Internet can improve the efficiency of students and teachers in finding desired information. In this WebQuest, you will study the latest techniques and strategies for searching the Internet and prepare a workshop for teachers to help them understand and use these tools and techniques.

The Task
You have been asked to give a workshop for your fellow teachers on how to find information on the Internet. You will prepare a PowerPoint presentation that will provide workshop participants with essential information and will guide you through your delivery.

 

Process and Resources

The World Wide Web contains over two billion Web sites and there is a plethora of electronic information that is not on the Web. An important question is, How do you find the information you want? Different people use different strategies, of course, and they also use different strategies in different situations. You probably already have a favorite strategy for finding what you want on the Web. You have the assignment of preparing a workshop for teachers who have only used the Internet on a limited basis and who can benefit from knowing about and understanding how to find what they want in the most efficient manner.

To understand and master strategies using Search Engines and other search tools, go to this Web site for a thorough and easy to understand tutorial. Click here.

After you have gone through the tutorial and feel that you understand the latest tools and techniques for searching the Internet and the Web, prepare a PowerPoint presentation for your workshop. Send this presentation as an attachment to your instructor.
