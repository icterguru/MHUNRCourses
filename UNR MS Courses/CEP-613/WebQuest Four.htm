WebQuest Four

Integrating Technology

Into the Classroom

Introduction

Whether or not the integration of information technology into the classroom is eff ective has been and remains a controversial issue. In this WebQuest, you will do some reading and Web browsing relative to this issue. You will then summarize your findings in a paper to support the position that integrating information technology into the classroom can enhance teaching and learning.

Task

In this WebQuest you will play the role of an upset educator who believes that modern information technology tools can enhance and improve teaching and learning. In your role, you will respond to a negative press statement by writing an editorial for your local news paper. Your editorial will provide evidence that information technology tools can be used effectively in education.

Process and Resources

Exercise A--Read the Following Statement
This is the press statement you will react to:
Current approaches to technology education, supported by CoSN, the International Society for Technology in Education (ISTE), and the U.S. Department of Education, emphasize the use of advanced technologies by every child and every teacher from kindergarten on up. Yet there is an appalling lack of research evidence justifying this massive and costly experiment, particularly if one looks for long-term, cost-effective gains. Just this year, Susan Patrick, head of the Office of Educational Technology at the Education Department, admitted that evidence for the effectiveness of high technology in education was lacking and that “despite a decade of investment, most achievement indicators are flat.”

Exercise C--Search the Web
The Web sites in Exercise B provide good background information to be used in your editorial, but of course, there is much more information to be found on the Web. Use your search skills to find other Web sites that will provide further evidence you can use to bolster your arguments.

Exercise D--Write and Editorial
Write a 3-5 page editorial (double-spaced) to support your position on the effective use of information technology in the classroom. Send your completed editorial to your instructor as an attachment. Use the following format to construct your editorial.

  1. State the main point of your argument (i.e., theses statement). Note: your argument is that the integration of information technology into the classroom is effective.
  2. Document examples to support your argument (reference the Web sites you use).
  3. Discuss the common tactics, strategies, or methodologies that are pervasive in successful uses of information technology in the classroom.
  4. Summarize your argument.
